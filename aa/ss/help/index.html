AA:SS Packet Hub

Packet index for Advanced Astronomy: Solar System

These packet pages are the working layer for AA:SS. The list is intentionally ordered, since the Solar System material has a more natural progression than AA:X: the course moves from the sky as observed, to historical geometric reasoning, to orbital dynamics, to the structure and formation of the Solar System, and then into detailed studies of the Sun and Jupiter.

For now, these packet pages are primarily navigation targets and future homes for notes, reminders, and packet-specific help. Over time, each page can grow into a place for clarifications, diagrams, common stumbling blocks, and links to related packets.

Packets

  1. Celestial Sphere Motion on the sky, seasons, eclipses, celestial coordinates, and practical angular reasoning.
  2. Aristarchus and Eratosthenes Relative sizes and distances in the Sun–Earth–Moon system, and how geometric reasoning leads to absolute scale.
  3. Kepler and Orbits Kepler’s laws, ellipses, the two-body problem, and the connection between orbital regularities and dynamics.
  4. Solar System Gross structure, formation, planetesimals, the nebular hypothesis, and the logic behind the present arrangement of the Solar System.
  5. The Sun Internal structure, luminosity, radiation, solar wind, magnetic activity, and fusion.
  6. Jupiter Rotating frames, pseudo-forces, zonal flow, oblateness, Lagrange points, and the Jovian system.

How to use this hub

If you are currently working in AA:SS, this page should become your main jumping-off point. Later, each packet page can include notes about what background it assumes, what often causes confusion, and where to go next if you want reinforcement or extension.

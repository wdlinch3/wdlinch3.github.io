AA:U Packet Hub

Packet index for Advanced Astronomy: Universe

These packet pages are the working layer for AA:U. The list is ordered because the Universe material has a recognizable conceptual progression: stars and basic stellar observables first, then interstellar matter and star birth, then stellar evolution, compact objects and explosive endpoints, galaxies, and cosmology. Students who enter AA:U without AA:SS may need to complete some AA:X material alongside or before parts of this sequence.

For now, these packet pages are primarily navigation targets and future homes for notes, reminders, and packet-specific help. Over time, each page can grow into a place for diagrams, formulas, clarifications, and FAQ-style guidance.

Packets

  1. Stars I Parsecs, magnitudes, luminosity, blackbody radiation, spectral classification, HR diagrams, and stellar scaling relations.
  2. Interstellar Matter and Star Birth Nebulae, hydrogen regions, the 21-cm line, tracers, collapse, and the conditions that lead to star formation.
  3. Stellar Evolution I Broad evolutionary branches, major stages, and the large-scale logic of stellar life cycles.
  4. Stellar Evolution II Shell burning, pressure support, fusion pathways, and the split between low-mass and high-mass evolution.
  5. Stellar Evolution III Stellar death, nucleosynthesis, supernovae, compact remnants, and related luminous phenomena.
  6. Stellar Death and Compact Objects Nucleosynthesis, supernovae, white dwarfs, neutron stars, black holes, and luminous high-energy phenomena.
  7. Galaxies Galactic morphology, rotation curves, mass distribution, dark matter, and distance indicators.
  8. Cosmology The standard cosmological model, expansion, inflation, nucleosynthesis, and the fate of the universe.

How to use this hub

If you are currently working in AA:U, this page should become your packet map. Later, each packet page can include “what you need to remember first,” “what students usually trip over,” and “what packet to read next” sections.

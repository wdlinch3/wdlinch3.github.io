Compactification

A layered path from hidden extra dimensions to flux backgrounds and covariant superstrings

{% include compactification_family_nav.html %}

String theory begins with a tension between mathematical consistency and direct experience: the theory is most naturally formulated in more than four dimensions, while the observed macroscopic world appears four-dimensional. Compactification is the attempt to resolve that tension without simply discarding the extra dimensions. The hidden directions are not treated as decorative additions; their geometry becomes part of the mechanism by which lower-dimensional physics is produced.

This page family is organized as a reading path. It begins with the elementary idea that geometry can hide dimensions, then moves toward the more serious question: how much of four-dimensional physics is forced by the consistency conditions of the higher-dimensional theory? From there the path leads to Calabi-Yau spaces, moduli, flux backgrounds, branes, holography, and the covariant-superstring problems represented in the archival FONDECYT material.

Readers coming from the broad expository side of the site may want to begin with A New Calculus for Physics and Once Upon a Time. Readers coming from the technical side may want to keep Effective actions in view, since flux compactification is one place where low-energy effective descriptions become especially important.

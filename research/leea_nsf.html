The goals of a quantum theory of gravity include the origin story of spacetime and matter. Basic questions such as the existence and nature of spacetime singularities and the big bang are still out of reach with our current understanding of string/M-theory. A manifestly background-independent formulation describing the low-energy physics would provide answers to these key questions, but it is currently not clear how one should construct such a description. We propose to investigate to what extent the interplay between manifest off-shell supersymmetry, linearly-realized on-shell supersymmetry, and non-linearly realized supersymmetry can be exploited to reconstruct infinitely many higher-derivative corrections to supergravity and potentially even a low-energy effective action.

For open bosonic string theory the exact answer to this question was shown by Metsaev and Tseytlin to be the non-linear electrodynamics theory of Born and Infeld[1][2]. This is a remarkable situation because the latter was constructed half a century before the theory for which it is the effective action! We currently find ourselves in a situation similar to that of Born and Infeld just before their discovery: We know there is a fundamental theory we call M with certain very specific properties, but we do not understand the quantum degrees of freedom defining it. Can we draw from our experience with strings and the Born–Infeld (BI) theory to understand the leading low-energy effective action (LEEA) of M-theory?

An immediate problem is that the BI action has so many nice properties that it is not obvious which one(s) to adopt in the generalization to quantum gravity. For example, an attractive feature of the BI theory is that the Coulomb singularities of the Maxwell field are no longer solutions. Perhaps a generalization to gravity should disallow the Schwarzschild singularity, maybe replacing it with a smooth Bardeen-like black hole[3]. Such a criterion is very difficult to use to construct an effective action, although many attempts have been made[4]. Another property of BI theory is that it is invariant under electromagnetic “duality” transformations[5]. Such a criterion would be much easier to apply, but it is not strong enough to fix the BI Lagrangian: there are infinitely many non-linear electrodynamics theories that are similarly invariant.

In a remarkable paper, Bagger and Galperin showed that the BI theory is singled out by partially breaking extended supersymmetry from 4D, N=2 to N=1 if the Goldstino is the gaugino of an abelian vector multiplet[6]. This theorem, as it were, can be rephrased as follows: starting with 4D, N=1 manifestly supersymmetric Maxwell theory, deforming it with the leading higher-derivative correction and requiring invariance under a second non-manifest supersymmetry uniquely leads to the 4D, N=1 BI Lagrangian. In a component formulation, supersymmetrizing the deformation would be carried out painstakingly order by order, but using off-shell N=1 superspace, Bagger and Galperin derive the Cecotti–Ferrara[7] Lagrangian analytically.

(Riemann)2 and (Riemann)4 invariants in 11D, N=1/8 superspace

We submit that there is an analogous potential for M-theory. Here, too, the leading LEEA effective action is believed to result from supersymmetrizing the (Riemann)4 corrections to the eleven-dimensional supergravity action. In this case, however, there is no (known) off-shell superspace with eleven-dimensional super-Poincaré symmetry in which to construct the effective action. To circumvent this problem, we have constructed a suitable eleven-dimensional off-shell superspace with a reduced structure group SL(2, C) × G2. In this formulation, 4 of the supersymmetries are off shell and manifest and the remaining 28 are realized linearly but close only on shell. Such a separation has several advantages over the alternatives, with the most important one being that all the constraints of the theory are solved in terms of a finite number of eleven-dimensional superfields that are irreducible representations of the ISO(4|4) × ISO(7) subalgebra of the full Poincaré superalgebra. It is in this superspace that we propose to construct the (Riemann)4 deformation.

The (Riemann)4 invariant deforms the auxiliary-field terms of the eleven-dimensional supergravity action. This deforms the supersymmetry transformations with curvature terms. Re-diagonalizing the auxiliary fields in the component action (equivalently, integrating out the auxiliary fields) is expected to generate an infinite series of higher-derivative corrections to the 2- and 8-derivative terms so that the resulting effective action is invariant under the deformed supersymmetry transformations. Determining how much (if not all) of the leading LEEA of M-theory this infinite series of terms describes would then be a matter of checking the resulting action against known higher-derivative terms available in the literature.

Leading higher-derivative deformation

In our most recent work, we have computed the supergeometry of eleven-dimensional supergravity needed to build the (Riemann)4 invariant[WIP]. As a proof of principle, we also constructed the analogous leading deformation for five-dimensional supergravity in[8], and—as luck would have it—this invariant can be continued to 11D. We therefore propose to carry out this extension.

Once the invariant is obtained, we still face the problem of diagonalizing the auxiliary-field sector and solving the resulting equations explicitly. This step may not be straightforward and could even be impossible in closed form. This is because, in principle, we could face unsolvable algebraic equations for these fields (for example, coupled quartics) or even find that the equations are differential. In order to prepare for potential complications, we propose to investigate the auxiliary-field mechanisms in simpler theories with non-polynomial effective actions. Examples include the Born–Infeld action in superspaces of dimension D>4 and the supersymmetric extension of the model of Perry and Schwarz for the interacting chiral 2-form on an M5-brane[9]. To date, neither of these theories has been constructed in superspace. Besides shedding light on the auxiliary-field problem for higher-derivative theories, these models are interesting in their own right as they describe D-branes and M-branes in various dimensions. Specifically, we would construct a formulation of the space-filling D9-brane of type I superstrings and the M5-brane, both in superspaces with 4 of the 16 supercharges off shell and manifest.

Ten-dimensional Born–Infeld action

The goal is to describe the ten-dimensional N=1 super-Born–Infeld action in terms of off-shell superfields. As before, no off-shell superspace formulation exists preserving the 10D, N=1 super-Poincaré symmetries, so we attempt to describe the theory in the superspace R10|4 with only 4 of the 16 supercharges manifest. In such a description, the Maxwell field is represented by a real unconstrained superfield V(x, θ, θ̄; y) and three chiral superfields Φi(x, θ; y), where i=1,2,3 is an SU(3) index, x and y are the 4+6 bosonic coordinates of 10D Minkowski space, and {θ, θ̄} are the 4 odd coordinates for the manifest N=1/4 of the supersymmetries. The Maxwell theory has two known deformations of its abelian gauge transformation: the non-abelian deformation gives 10D super-Yang–Mills as formulated in this superspace by Marcus, Sagnotti, and Siegel[10] (and rediscovered by the phenomenology community much later in[11]). The other known deformation is the higher-derivative deformation that should lead to the 10D, N=1 Born–Infeld theory, which we propose to construct.

In preliminary work, we have already demonstrated the appropriate action for D=6. Due to the low complex “co-dimension” (D−4)/2 = 1, this action is a relatively straightforward modification of the 4D, N=1 action constructed by Cecotti and Ferrara[7]. In this case, there is a Fayet–Iliopoulos-type modification of the auxiliary-field interactions that correctly reproduces the additional √(1+F) factor.

The higher co-dimension cases are all essentially equivalent to 10D. In this language, there are new contributions to the bosonic field strength corresponding to the SU(3) ⊂ Spin(6) representations of dimensions 1, 3, 3̄, and 8. The first of these is the trace of the (1,1) part of the field strength extending the contribution found already in D=6, but to account for the others, a new auxiliary-field mechanism is needed. Especially the proper accounting of the 8 is mysterious, as there is no auxiliary field corresponding to such a representation. It is important to the M-theory project to understand in detail how this works out correctly, because a similar situation there involves the part of the Weyl tensor in the 70′ representation of G2.

Self-interacting 6D, N=(2,0) tensor multiplet

A potentially simpler example of an exact leading LEEA is the model of Perry and Schwarz for the chiral gauge 2-form, that is, a gauge 2-form in 6D with a self-dual field strength[9]. Such a field is the primary ingredient of the 6D, N=(2,0) tensor multiplet living on the world-volume of the M5-brane, so the non-linear theory is the 2-form analog of the Born–Infeld theory. In the approach of Perry and Schwarz, the chirality constraint is solved by reducing the structure group from SO(5,1) → SO(4,1). The chirality equation separates in such a way that it can be solved in terms of an unconstrained 6D field that is a 2-form of the Lorentz subgroup. A non-linear action for this field is then constructed by demanding that it realize the full 6D Poincaré symmetry non-linearly. We note that this approach is more than similar to the superspace approach advocated throughout this proposal, as both solve a constraint that is apparently only possible by reducing the structure group.

We would like to understand this model in superspace, not only because it is an effective action of the type central to this proposal, but also because it is the non-linear “electro-dynamics” of the single M5-brane. The appropriate superspace for this is R6|4 with 6D bosonic coordinates and 4 odd coordinates, because analogously to the bosonic situation, we need to split the 6D spinor in half, so that we can solve one half in terms of the other.

In preliminary work in this direction, we have constructed an action analogous to that of Cecotti and Ferrara that reduces in the 2-form sector to the action of Perry and Schwarz. There is no interesting auxiliary-field mechanism analogous to that of super-Born–Infeld theory; however, there is a curious normal-ordering ambiguity that affects the precise form of the fermionic sector and that we do not currently understand. We expect that this ambiguity is fixed by a proper accounting of the isometries of the theory. These isometries appear to be very closely related to the non-manifest symmetries in the off-shell superspace construction of eleven-dimensional supergravity described previously. If this connection can be made precise, it would essentially amount to a coupling of the super-Perry–Schwarz theory to eleven-dimensional supergravity. Reversing this procedure suggests that it may be possible to understand the Perry–Schwarz model for the chiral 2-form directly as a decoupling limit of eleven-dimensional supergravity in M-theory.

Finally, we note that this tensor multiplet is one of the main ingredients in the 6D, N=(1,0) non-anomalous matter–gravity systems. As such, its coupling to 6D, N=(1,0) supergravity and the construction of the latter in our reduced superspaces may be facilitated by the observations made here, or vice versa.
